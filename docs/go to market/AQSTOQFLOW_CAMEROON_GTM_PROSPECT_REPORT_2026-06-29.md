# AqStoqFlow Cameroon Go-To-Market Prospect Report - 2026-06-29

## Executive View

AqStoqFlow should not open Cameroon with a generic "ERP for everyone" pitch. The strongest wedge is: daily stock, cash, supplier, payroll, and accountant-ready evidence for owner-managed SMBs that have outgrown Excel and simple POS but are not ready for heavyweight enterprise ERP.

The best early adopters are operators with visible inventory movement, branch/location complexity, and owners who already feel cash leakage, stock-outs, staff accountability, supplier debt, and month-end accounting pressure.

Important caution: this is a prospecting report, not a confirmed sales pipeline. Public facts support the existence/category of named prospects, but willingness to buy must be validated by direct discovery.

## Cameroon Acceptance Strategy

### Beachhead Segment

Start with two beachheads:

1. Supermarkets, mini-markets, and wholesale distributors: visible stock/cash pain, clear owner ROI, and strong accounting evidence needs.
2. Hardware/building-material and pharmacy operators: high-value inventory, supplier credit, and strong control pressure.

Avoid beginning with highly regulated statutory payroll or complex tax automation as the primary promise. Use OHADA/accounting-readiness as evidence positioning, not as a legal guarantee.

### Trust Channels

- Accountants and bookkeeping firms: make them allies by giving them cleaner source documents, not by threatening to replace them.
- POS hardware vendors and receipt-printer suppliers: bundle AqStoqFlow as the management layer above devices.
- Business associations and market clusters: run small demos for groups, especially retail/wholesale clusters.
- Mall tenant communities: use Douala Grand Mall and Playce-style tenant density for field demos.
- Local entrepreneur media and LinkedIn/Facebook business communities: publish short proof stories, not abstract SaaS promises.

### Demo Strategy

1. Owner daily truth demo: yesterday's sales, cash expected vs counted, top stock risks, supplier obligations, and actions.
2. Accountant/OHADA evidence demo: show sales, purchases, cash close, payroll/payment evidence, and exportable proof trail without claiming certification.
3. Inventory/cash leakage demo: show item movement from receipt to sale to variance to owner alert.

### Pilot Offer

Recommended pilot: 30 days, paid but low-risk.

- Scope: one branch/location, one owner dashboard, one cashier workflow, one storekeeper workflow, one accountant export.
- Modules: POS/cash close, inventory, supplier/AP, basic finance analytics, accountant pack.
- Price logic: small setup fee plus discounted monthly fee; waive part of setup if the client agrees to provide a testimonial after success.
- Success metrics: stock variance reduced, daily cash close completed, supplier balance visible, accountant receives cleaner monthly pack, owner checks dashboard at least 3 times/week.
- Exit path: if metrics are not visible in 30 days, stop; if visible, expand to more branches or modules.

### Field Sales Motion

1. Map 30 prospects by sector and city.
2. Ask for a 20-minute operational pain interview, not a software demo first.
3. Bring a printed one-page French/English pitch.
4. Ask for their current pain: stock loss, cash variance, supplier debt, accountant delays.
5. Demo only the workflow matching their pain.
6. Offer one-branch pilot with written success criteria.
7. Include the accountant/bookkeeper in the second meeting.
8. Train cashier, storekeeper, manager, and accountant separately.
9. Send a weekly pilot proof report.
10. Close expansion only after showing measurable daily truth.

### Content And Visibility

- WhatsApp clips: 45-second demos for daily cash close, low-stock alert, supplier debt, and accountant pack.
- One-page sector sheets: supermarket, pharmacy, hardware, wholesale, bakery/restaurant.
- Before/after Excel story: show how the same day looks in notebook/Excel versus AqStoqFlow.
- Accountant checklist: "10 proofs your client should produce monthly."
- Daily cash truth calculator: estimate leakage from small unclosed variances.
- Founder-led demos: local trust matters; use direct, practical language.
- Case-study promise: after 30 days, publish anonymized "before/after" numbers with client consent.

### Product Acceptance Strategy

- Keep onboarding simple: import items/customers/suppliers from Excel.
- Provide sector templates: mini-market, pharmacy, hardware, bakery, wholesale.
- Make role dashboards clear: owner, cashier, storekeeper, accountant.
- Support low-connectivity workflows where possible.
- Use FCFA everywhere.
- Keep French first, English ready.
- Make the accountant pack visible from week one.
- Build a weekly owner ritual: Monday review of stock risks, cash variance, supplier exposure, and action list.

## Final Recommendations

Top 3 prospects to approach first:
1. DOVV Distribution - best local-chain proof story.
2. Quifeurou - strongest inventory-value and branch-control pain.
3. Douala Grand Mall / Playce tenant clusters - fastest path to many SMB conversations in one physical place.

First 10 outreach actions:
1. Prepare a bilingual one-page pitch.
2. Prepare 3 sector demos: mini-market, hardware, pharmacy.
3. Build a 14-day sample dashboard using demo data.
4. Ask accountants for 10 owner-managed clients with stock/cash pain.
5. Visit Douala Grand Mall and Playce tenants physically.
6. Identify DOVV/Santa Lucia/Mahima operations contacts through warm introductions.
7. Create WhatsApp demo clips.
8. Offer 5 controlled pilots, not unlimited demos.
9. Track every objection in a CRM/list.
10. Convert the first success into an anonymized proof story.

Best pilot offer: 30-day one-branch operating truth pilot: inventory + cash close + supplier/AP + accountant pack + owner dashboard.

Most dangerous objection: "We already have POS/accountant/Excel." Response: "Good. AqStoqFlow does not start by replacing them; it proves whether the owner and accountant can trust daily operations."

How to prove value in 14 days:
- complete daily cash close for 10 business days;
- detect at least 5 stock exceptions;
- produce supplier exposure list;
- produce one accountant-ready weekly pack;
- show owner dashboard usage.

Features that must be strongest before outreach:
- inventory and stock movement truth;
- POS/cash close;
- supplier/AP basics;
- role-based access;
- French-first UI;
- Excel import;
- clean accountant/export evidence;
- simple onboarding.

Claims to avoid until reviewed:
- do not claim full legal/tax compliance automation;
- do not claim certified OHADA filings;
- do not claim payroll statutory production readiness for all cases;
- do not claim replacement of the accountant;
- do not claim guaranteed theft prevention.
