# 10 High-Value Business Intelligence Capabilities KONTAVA Should Adopt from Leading Business Platforms

Date: 2026-06-22

## Slide 1: Executive Summary

KONTAVA should treat Business Intelligence as a product capability, not only as a reporting layer. The strongest platforms use BI to help users know what is happening, decide what to do next, detect risk early, improve revenue, and trust the platform because every number is traceable.

The benchmark platforms show a clear pattern:

- BI is embedded inside daily workflows, not hidden in a separate analytics area.
- Dashboards must be role-based: founder, finance, sales, operations, compliance, and end user views should answer different questions.
- The most valuable BI combines operational data, financial data, customer behavior, risk signals, and recommendations.
- Mature BI moves from "what happened" to "what changed, why it changed, what will happen next, and what action should be taken."

For KONTAVA, the opportunity is to build an intelligent operating layer for business users: dashboards, scorecards, alerts, forecasts, audit trails, and AI-assisted recommendations that help each user run their business better.

## Slide 3: What The Best Platforms Have In Common

- They turn raw business activity into trusted decision surfaces.
- They use dashboards as command centers, not decoration.
- They combine data governance with speed: real-time where needed, reconciled where necessary.
- They give each role a focused view of the business.
- They connect insight to next action: workflow, alert, recommendation, rule, or forecast.
- They let users drill from a KPI into the source records behind it.
- They protect trust with permissions, auditability, and clear definitions.

## Slide 17: Product Architecture Principles For KONTAVA BI

- Build a governed metric layer before building many dashboards.
- Every KPI should have a definition, owner, data source, refresh policy, and permission policy.
- Use tenant-scoped read models so one business never sees another business's data.
- Keep financial and compliance numbers traceable to ledger entries, documents, and source transactions.
- Separate real-time operational signals from reconciled financial reports.
- Add drilldowns from every aggregate to the records behind the number.
- Treat alerts as workflow items with owner, status, severity, resolution, and audit trail.
- Design BI for low-friction daily use: mobile-friendly, role-aware, and action-oriented.

## Slide 18: Strategic Recommendations

1. Start with dashboards that answer the hardest daily business questions: "How much money did we make?", "Where is cash stuck?", "Which stock is at risk?", "Who needs attention?", and "What must I do today?"
2. Build KONTAVA BI around trusted source records, not vanity charts.
3. Prioritize finance, sales, inventory, and risk because these create the highest user value and platform trust.
4. Add forecasting only after the historical metric layer is reliable.
5. Make BI embedded: POS, invoices, inventory, customer profiles, and accounting screens should show the relevant insight at the point of action.
6. Design every recommendation with an explanation: why this was suggested, what data supports it, and what action is available.
7. Package advanced BI as a premium KONTAVA tier after the core dashboard foundation is proven.
