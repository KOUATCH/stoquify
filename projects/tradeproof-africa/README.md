# TradeProof Africa

Saved: 2026-06-28

## Executive Summary

TradeProof Africa is a compliance and traceability operating system for African exporters, cooperatives, processors, and EU buyers who must prove that covered commodities are legal and deforestation-free.

The strongest wedge is not a generic agriculture app, marketplace, or fintech tool. It is a market-access survival product for exporters and buyers facing hard regulatory pressure, urgent evidence requirements, and severe commercial downside if compliance fails.

## Recommended App Idea

Build a platform that turns farm, stock, and shipment data into buyer-ready compliance evidence packs for commodities affected by the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), including cocoa, coffee, rubber, palm oil, cattle, soy, wood, and certain derived products.

Positioning:

> Keep African exporters inside premium global markets by turning farm, stock, and shipment data into trusted compliance evidence.

## Why This Is Attractive

### Huge Problem

African exporters and their buyers need credible proof that covered goods are:

- deforestation-free
- produced in line with local laws in the country of production
- traceable to the relevant production locations
- supported by auditable due-diligence evidence

The European Commission states that EUDR requirements apply from:

- 30 December 2026 for large and medium operators
- 30 June 2027 for micro and small operators
- 30 December 2026 for micro and small operators already covered by the EU Timber Regulation

Non-EU producers may not have direct obligations unless they place products on the EU market, but EU companies can still request location and production information from them to satisfy EUDR requirements.

### Strong Willingness To Pay

The buyer's pain is not "nice to have." If an exporter or EU buyer cannot produce compliance evidence, they risk blocked shipments, buyer rejection, penalties, delayed payment, loss of contracts, and exclusion from premium supply chains.

This creates a high-intensity B2B pain point with a clear budget owner:

- exporters
- processors
- cooperatives
- EU importers
- commodity buyers
- audit and certification firms
- trade-finance providers

### Rare Niche With Wide Reach

The niche is rare because it sits at the intersection of:

- African agricultural export chains
- regulatory compliance
- geolocation and farm mapping
- batch traceability
- buyer due diligence
- trade finance readiness

But the reach is wide because the same compliance infrastructure can expand across countries, commodities, buyers, and regulations.

## Target Customer

Do not start by selling directly to individual farmers. Start with the actors who lose money if compliance fails:

- exporters and processors
- cooperatives and aggregators
- EU importers and commodity buyers
- certification and audit firms
- banks and trade-finance providers
- later, governments and AfCFTA trade-support programs

The cooperative/exporter layer is the practical entry point because it already aggregates farmers, controls shipment workflows, and has direct buyer pressure.

## Core Product

The MVP should generate a compliance evidence pack for a shipment.

Core modules:

1. Farmer and plot registry
2. GPS point and polygon capture
3. Offline mobile field-agent app
4. Batch traceability from farm to warehouse to shipment
5. Deforestation risk screening using satellite or public map data
6. Local legality checklist for land, permits, tax, labor, and production rules
7. Document and photo evidence vault
8. Buyer portal with shipment-level evidence
9. Audit trail with timestamps, role permissions, and signed records
10. Exportable due-diligence packet for buyers and auditors

Do not start with blockchain. Start with an append-only audit ledger, signed records, timestamps, permissioned workflows, and exportable evidence packs. Blockchain can be added later only if buyers specifically demand it.

## Implementation Strategy

### Phase 1: Pick One Beachhead

Best initial wedges:

- cocoa in Cote d'Ivoire or Ghana
- coffee in Ethiopia, Uganda, Rwanda, or Kenya
- rubber or palm oil where exporter networks are easier to access

Selection criteria:

- easy access to exporters and cooperatives
- strong EU buyer exposure
- urgent traceability pressure
- feasible field mapping
- existing documents that can be digitized quickly

### Phase 2: Validate Willingness To Pay

Run interviews before building too much.

Interview targets:

- 20 exporters or processors
- 10 cooperatives or aggregators
- 10 EU importers or commodity buyers
- 3-5 auditors, certifiers, or compliance consultants

Questions to ask:

- What happens if you cannot provide farm/location evidence?
- What documents do buyers ask for today?
- What is the current cost per farmer, cooperative, batch, or shipment?
- What tools are already used?
- Where does compliance currently break?
- Would you pay per farmer, per shipment, per buyer, or annually?
- Who signs the purchase order?

### Phase 3: MVP In 8-12 Weeks

Build only the narrow evidence-pack workflow:

- organization and user roles
- farmer registry
- farm plot capture
- batch intake and movement records
- shipment builder
- evidence upload
- basic geospatial risk flag
- buyer-facing evidence page
- PDF/ZIP export
- audit log

Success criteria:

- a field agent can register a farmer and plot offline
- a cooperative can connect farmers to a batch
- an exporter can connect batches to a shipment
- a buyer can review an evidence pack
- the system can export a packet that is useful for due diligence

### Phase 4: Pilot

Run a controlled pilot with:

- 1-2 exporters
- 5-10 cooperatives
- 1,000-5,000 farmers
- 1-3 buyer review workflows

Pilot metrics:

- percentage of farms mapped
- percentage of batches linked to farms
- time to produce an evidence pack
- buyer acceptance rate
- cost per mapped farmer
- number of compliance blockers found before shipment
- shipment delay reduction

### Phase 5: Expand

After the first beachhead works, expand into:

- more commodities
- more countries
- buyer API integrations
- auditor workflows
- satellite monitoring partnerships
- trade-finance risk scoring
- AfCFTA and export-document readiness
- sustainability premium tracking

## Business Model

Recommended pricing model:

- exporter SaaS subscription
- plus per mapped farmer or active farm plot
- plus per shipment evidence pack
- optional buyer portal fee
- optional auditor/certifier workspace fee
- optional premium integrations for satellite, ERP, or finance partners

Example packaging:

- Starter: one exporter, limited cooperatives, manual evidence export
- Growth: multi-cooperative workflow, buyer portal, audit logs, batch traceability
- Enterprise: API, custom buyer requirements, finance integrations, advanced geospatial risk, dedicated compliance templates

## Defensibility

The moat is not the software screens. The moat is the verified evidence graph:

- farmers
- plots
- cooperatives
- batches
- warehouses
- shipments
- buyers
- documents
- risk flags
- audit decisions

Over time, this creates trusted compliance history that can support buyer confidence, faster audits, trade finance, insurance, and premium-market access.

## Risks

Main risks:

- regulation changes or simplification reduces urgency
- field mapping is expensive and operationally messy
- exporters may expect buyers or donors to pay
- existing certification firms may bundle similar software
- satellite deforestation checks can produce disputes or false positives
- data ownership and privacy concerns can slow adoption

Mitigations:

- sell market access and buyer acceptance, not only regulation
- start with the exporter/cooperative layer, not individual farmers
- build offline-first workflows
- keep evidence exportable so buyers can trust the system quickly
- partner with auditors instead of trying to replace them
- add finance and buyer workflows after traceability is proven

## Immediate Next Steps

1. Choose the first country and commodity.
2. Create a one-page buyer/explorer pitch.
3. Interview 30-40 target customers.
4. Map current compliance workflow from farm registration to shipment.
5. Build a clickable prototype of the evidence pack and buyer portal.
6. Pilot with one exporter before expanding the product surface.
